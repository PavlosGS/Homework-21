Description: Trail Buddy is a brain child of mine, for an app that can allow Hikers to notify each others about dangerour or interesting plants and animals on the trail.

Problem Hypothesis: An app that helps hikers identify plants and animals on the trail using AI as well as to allow them to report live these sightings or warn others of potential dangers will be very useful.

Project Team: Pavlos Savva (UX Designer & visual & front end), Ethan Richard, Juliette Hartel, JaQue Latham

Tools used: Figma, FigJam, Photoshop, Illustrator, G-Suite, Visual Studio, GitHub, Trello, Zoom

User Research

As a team we developed a user interview plan and an online survey in order to to get enough qualitative and quantitative data to validate our problem hypothesis. We performed 6 interviews, and I had the opportunity to conduct 2 of them. 34 people took our survey as well. The insights we got where confirming our hypothesis, that hikers like to have real time updates about sightings on the trail and have the desire to learn more about the wildlife they encounter

The Insights from this research helped us define our user, the actual problem, and also the value proposition for our app.

We discovered that people go hiking as a way to unwind at the end of their day, but they also want to know about dangerous hiking contitions on the trail.

Definition

Problem Statement: Jade Newsome, needs access to time conscious updates on trail conditions so she can plan more effectively, and ways to learn more about plants and animals she encounters on the trail.

Value Proposition: Our App Trail Buddy, provides hikers with the ability to notify other about dangers or interesting plants and animals they encounter while on the trail. This is also leveraged by AI which provides an easy plant and animal identification information quickly and easily.

Ideation

Jade and her friend are hiking together during the weekend. She uses TrailBuddy to look through her favorites list for a trail that has light foot traffic on the weekend. She picks one, prepares her list of hiking essentials for that trail, and shares it with her friends.

Jade and her friends start hiking and TrailBuddy alerts them that the trail ahead is muddy. Jade and her friends pick a new path to avoid the mud. Later on, they encounter a snake on the trail and report it using the Ping feature. They also use the Snap ID feature to share photos of a bird’s nest with the community. They have a great time hiking, rate the trail, and give the new version of TrailBuddy 5 stars.

Our opportunities focus on creating a way for hikers to plan for their hikes, share sightings and identify wildlife while on the trail.

Usability Testing

We performed Guerilla usability testing with 3 users and I performed 1 of those tests.

Test 1:Picking a trail from the Home page.
User's needed a little help selecting one of the flags.

Task 2:Placing a ping on the trail.
This was fairly easy for most users, but did not immediatetly understant the difference between the danger icons and the regular sightings icons.

Task 3:Using the Snap ID feature to Identify plants and animals
There was no particular hesitation with this feature and it seemed to be easy for users to understand.

Task 4:Setting the account preferences.
The users were not clear on some of the language in this section as it relates to the range of time that trail activity is visible.

Test 1 changes

  • Added a tooltip
  • Also implemented part of the search function for the next iteration

Test 2 changes

  • Included a red color and an exclamation mark for the danger icons and Green color for the regular sightings icons.

Test 3 changes

  • No changes here, except the use of style guide visual design and colors for the Hifi version.

Test 4 changes

  • Adjusted the language to better explain how the users are supposed to use the range of time for the trail notifications
